# brentcarlin.com — Copy Style Guide

> Reference for all case study SEO copy. Updated as style is refined through iteration.

---

## Voice

Minimal. Confident. Specific. The work speaks — copy frames it without overselling.  
Emotional connection comes through detail and restraint, not drama.

---

## Sentence Construction

- **Direct openers** — lead with the subject and a strong verb. Cut setup phrases.
  - ✓ `Animation was the core feature`
  - ✗ `The animation was the experience —`

- **Comma flow over em dashes** — lets sentences breathe without dramatic weight.
  - ✓ `Animation was the core feature, a horizontal parallax side scroller letting the product story unfold`
  - ✗ `The animation was the experience — a custom horizontal parallax that moved sideways`

- **Lean sentence openers** — strip "built to be", "designed to", "created as" when the verb can stand alone.
  - ✓ `Responsive across early mobile devices and legacy browsers`
  - ✗ `Built to be responsive across early mobile devices and legacy browsers`

---

## Word Choice

- **Simple noun phrases** — name the thing plainly.
  - ✓ `product story`
  - ✗ `mascara's lengthening effect`

- **Specifics beat generics** — always use the real name, place, or number when available.
  - ✓ `in-store QR placements at Walmart`
  - ✗ `QR code placements in-store`

- **Audience context early** — if the audience shapes the work, say so in sentence one.
  - ✓ `a landing page built for a young audience`

---

## Structure Per Case Study

```
Sentence 1 — What was built and who it was for (audience or brand context)
Sentence 2 — The core feature or technical approach (animation, interaction, architecture)
Sentence 3 — How it held up (responsiveness, compatibility, scale, craft)
Sentence 4 — Outcome (metric + context, specific retailer/placement if relevant)
```

3–4 sentences. 4 is the ceiling.

---

## Tags

5–7 tags. Mix:
- Stack / technology (`JavaScript`, `GSAP`, `React`, `Next.js`)
- Interaction type (`Parallax`, `Scroll Animation`, `WebGL`)
- Project type (`Campaign Landing Page`, `Web App`, `Brand Experience`)
- Positioning (`Responsive Web Design`, `Performance`, `Headless CMS`)

---

## What to Avoid

- Em dashes for dramatic effect
- "needed more than", "wasn't just", "more than a product page" — overselling
- Adjective stacking — if it doesn't add meaning, cut it
- Vague outcome lines — always pair metrics with context (platform, duration, channel)

---

## Reference Example (Covergirl Supersizer — approved)

**Headline:** Motion as product story — Covergirl Supersizer.

**Tags:** `JavaScript` `Parallax` `Scroll Animation` `Campaign Landing Page` `Responsive Web Design` `Mobile` `GSAP`

**Body Copy:**
Covergirl's Supersizer campaign needed a landing page built for a young audience. Animation was the core feature, a horizontal parallax side scroller letting the product story unfold without interrupting native interactions. Responsive across early mobile devices and legacy browsers, the experience held consistently wherever users landed. The campaign earned 40,000+ impressions over three months through in-store QR placements at Walmart.
